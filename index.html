    Do you own land in or near the proposed corridors in the map and would you be willing to have some trees and shrubs planted or glider poles erected (at no expense to you)? If so, we'd love to hear from you.

    Most of the Scenic Rim's national parks and other protected areas harbour high-altitude dense forest. Squirrel gliders and other wildlife prefer low-altitude, drier open forests which are not so well protected. Movement between remaining forest fragments is becoming increasingly difficult. We'd like to change that!

    You can check to see where your prpoerty lies in respect to the proposed corridors. Simply search by your property name in the search box in the top right corner of the map. If its not included, no problem, just get in touch via the Contact Us tab above and let us know your address - we'll work it out from there.

    Application features
    • This web mapping application was developed by BioGeo in support of the Wildlife Preservation Society Queensland. It's a full screen mobile-friendly application with responsive navbar and modal placeholders using Bootstrap 3, Leaflet, and typeahead.js, and builds on the excellent Bootleaf code.

      Features include client-side multi-layer search with autocomplete, and responsive sidebar synced to the map bounds that can be sorted or filtered.

    The data provided on this site is for conservation purposes only and to assist Wildlife Preservation Society Queensland in managing this project.

    Absolutely no accuracy or completeness is guaranteed, implied or intended. All property information was obtained via open access sources via the Queensland Spatial Catalogue - QSpatial.

    Properties data courtesy of the Queensland Spatial Catalogue - QSpatial. The polygons represent privately owned rural properties or land parcels that can be searched for by property name.

    Proposed coridors within this application show very approximate preliminary plans for corridor routes and are being developed and managed by Wildlife Quensland - Scenic Rim branch

    Hubs represent refuges for our target wildlife and are points from which our proposed corridors branch off from. Please contaact Wildlife Quensland - Scenic Rim branch for further information

    Centroids data equate to the centre point of each property/land parcel.

    Species data (human observed accounts between 2015-2020) obtained from the Global Biodiversity Information Facility
